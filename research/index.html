§ 01 — Dissecting the incumbent

What Liquid Glass actually does

It's not "blur plus transparency." The 2013 flattening took depth away; Liquid Glass puts a very specific kind back — not skeuomorphic texture, but optical material. Five mechanics carry the whole language.

Real-time refraction

Edges lens and magnify the content behind them like the rim of a real glass lens — not a flat backdrop blur. The bend is strongest at curved edges, falling off toward center.

Specular light response

A moving highlight rides each control's rim, shifting with device tilt and with content scrolling underneath. The material reads as physically present, not painted.

Adaptive legibility

The layer samples what's behind it every frame and re-tints itself — and the text on top — so labels stay readable over any wallpaper or video. The material is aware of its background.

Concentric squircles

Built on continuous-curvature corners with shared nested radii. Glass pieces are lozenge-shaped and morph fluidly between states instead of hard-cutting.

Fluid morphing motion

Tab bars shrink on scroll, buttons swell on press, sheets stretch like a viscous liquid. The "liquid" lives in the motion model as much as the look.

The cracks that open the door. Liquid Glass is GPU-expensive (refraction + adaptive sampling every frame), carries real accessibility friction (contrast, motion), and breeds sameness — once every app is a frosted pane, the material differentiates no one. It also leans on Apple's compositor; faithful web reproduction is costly and approximate. That is exactly the gap a next trend walks through.
