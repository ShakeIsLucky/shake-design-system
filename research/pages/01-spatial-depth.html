Beyond the flat sheet

Glass was a window. STRATA is the room behind it.

Every element is assigned a depth value and rendered on its own plane. The compositor handles perspective, occlusion, and depth-of-field automatically — so your UI breathes in three dimensions.

01

True Z-stacking

Assign any node a depth from -1 to +1. STRATA places it in real perspective space — closer layers loom, farther ones recede and soften.

02

Reactive camera

Cursor and scroll drive a live perspective camera. The whole composition shifts viewpoint with motion that never feels gimmicky — just spatial.

03

Depth-of-field

A real focal plane keeps your focus crisp while background strata blur progressively — the cinematic cue your eyes already trust.

The compositor

Drag a layer back, watch it leave focus.

These four slabs sit at different depths in a single perspective stage. As your cursor moves they parallax against each other — near slabs sweep fast, far slabs drift slow and soft. That separation is the whole point: depth your hands can read.
